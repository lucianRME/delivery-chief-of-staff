# Business Impact

## Target Users

- Programme Managers.
- Delivery Managers.
- PMO and Governance teams.
- Release Managers.
- Senior delivery leaders.

## Current-State Pain

Delivery assurance depends on information distributed across Jira, RAID logs, and release plans.
Before a weekly governance review, teams manually filter work items, compare dependencies, check
owners and mitigations, assess release controls, and turn the result into an executive narrative.

This process commonly takes several hours and relies heavily on reviewer experience. Checks can be
applied inconsistently, the same issue may be counted more than once, and missing ownership or
mitigation can remain unnoticed. Leadership may receive a large status pack without a concise
explanation of the decisions required.

## Future-State Benefit

Delivery Chief of Staff automates the first pass of delivery assurance. Users upload existing CSV
exports and receive:

- Consistent risk, dependency, governance, and release-readiness checks.
- A deduplicated and capped Delivery Health Score.
- Evidence-backed findings and prioritised recommendations.
- A concise executive summary focused on leadership action.
- A downloadable report with a complete evidence and audit trail.

The result is faster preparation and a more consistent basis for governance. Programme professionals
retain accountability for judgement and decisions while spending less time assembling the underlying
evidence.

## Measurable Impact Hypotheses

- Reduce weekly delivery review preparation effort by **30–50%**.
- Reduce the time required to identify governance gaps from hours to minutes.
- Improve consistency in RAID and release-governance checks across programmes.
- Accelerate leadership decision-making by presenting prioritised issues and explicit decision
  prompts.
- Reduce missed ownership and mitigation gaps through systematic checks of every supplied record.

These are testable hypotheses for a pilot, not assumed production outcomes. Baselines should be
established with participating delivery teams before measuring impact.

## Banking Operations Impact

Delivery Chief of Staff is positioned for banking operations and shared services governance, where
delivery reviews must be consistent, explainable, and suitable for management oversight.

It supports:

- **Shared services governance:** repeatable review of delivery artefacts across programmes and
  teams.
- **Delivery assurance:** consistent detection of delivery risk, ownership gaps, and mitigation
  gaps.
- **Release readiness:** checks for milestones, entry and exit criteria, rollback plans,
  communications, and business approval.
- **Operational control checks:** systematic review of ownership, mitigations, acceptance criteria,
  dependency ownership, and readiness controls.
- **Exception handling:** clear identification of blocked work, high-impact RAID items,
  dependencies, and at-risk milestones.
- **Leadership reporting:** concise executive summary, decision prompt, and prioritised actions.
- **Auditability:** stable evidence keys and a downloadable report linking conclusions back to
  source records.

Cautious measurable impact hypotheses for banking operations:

- 30–50% reduction in delivery review preparation effort.
- Governance gaps identified in minutes rather than hours.
- More consistent ownership, mitigation, and release-readiness checks.
- Faster leadership intervention on delivery exceptions.
- Improved audit readiness through evidence-backed findings.

## Example Before and After

### Before

A Programme Manager manually reviews a Jira export, RAID log, and release plan. The team spends
several hours reconciling records, identifying gaps, preparing slides, and checking that conclusions
can be traced to source evidence.

### After

The Programme Manager uploads the three CSV files and receives a Delivery Health Score, prioritised
risks, dependencies, governance findings, recommended actions, executive summary, and downloadable
report in under a minute.

The remaining review time is used to validate context, agree ownership, and make decisions rather
than assemble information.

## Enterprise Relevance

The solution addresses recurring needs in enterprise delivery governance:

- **Delivery assurance:** consistent review of delivery risk and execution signals.
- **Release readiness:** systematic checks of milestones, entry and exit criteria, rollback plans,
  and approvals.
- **RAID management:** early identification of high-impact items, missing owners, and missing
  mitigations.
- **Governance:** a repeatable assessment that supports programme and portfolio forums.
- **Auditability:** stable evidence keys and a report trail connecting conclusions to Jira, RAID,
  and release records.

The local, file-based MVP also lowers adoption barriers for a pilot because it works with familiar
exports and requires no integration project.

## Success Metrics

- Average delivery-review preparation time saved per programme per week.
- Number and severity of delivery risks detected.
- Number of missing owners identified across Jira, RAID, and release records.
- Number of missing mitigation plans identified.
- Number of release-readiness gaps identified before governance checkpoints.
- Time from issue identification to leadership decision.
- Stakeholder satisfaction with the clarity and usefulness of executive reporting.
